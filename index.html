Circulatory System

Transports blood, nutrients, oxygen, and removes waste across the body.

Key parts

  • Heart — pump with 4 chambers (right/left atria, right/left ventricles).
  • Arteries — carry blood away from the heart.
  • Veins — carry blood back to the heart.
  • Capillaries — tiny vessels for exchange.
Pulse points
  1. Radial (wrist)
  2. Carotid (neck)
  3. Brachial (inner elbow)
Fun fact

Your heart beats about 100,000 times per day.

Respiratory System

Intakes oxygen, expels carbon dioxide, and enables gas exchange in the lungs.

Key parts

  • Nose / Nasal cavity
  • Pharynx & Larynx
  • Trachea & Bronchi
  • Alveoli — site of gas exchange
  • Diaphragm — main breathing muscle
How gas exchange works

Oxygen passes from alveoli into blood capillaries; CO₂ moves from blood to alveoli to be exhaled.

Did you know?

Adult lungs can hold several liters of air depending on the person and activity.

Pulse Points & Quick Facts

Radial pulse

Located at the wrist — easy to check heart rate.

Carotid pulse

At the neck — used in emergencies to check circulation.

Normal adult resting heart rate

About 60–100 beats per minute for most adults.
